Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best restaurant app in 2026?

It depends on what you need. For discovery, Google Maps is the most reliable. For reservations, OpenTable has the widest network. For trendy spots, Resy. And if you want to find restaurants that don't pressure you for tips, SkipATip is the only app built specifically for that.

Is there an app to find restaurants without tip screens?

Yes — SkipATip (skipatip.com) is a verified directory of restaurants that don't use tip-pressure screens. Every listing pays a $49 verification fee to confirm they're genuinely tip-free. It's the only app of its kind.

Which restaurant app has the most accurate reviews?

Google Maps tends to have the most trustworthy reviews because they're tied to real Google accounts and harder to fake. Yelp has more reviews overall but has faced criticism for its filtering algorithm. For fine dining, OpenTable and Resy reviews come from verified diners who actually ate there.

Do restaurant apps charge restaurants to be listed?

It varies. Google Maps and Yelp are free to claim a listing, but Yelp charges for advertising. OpenTable and Resy charge per-cover fees to restaurants. SkipATip charges a one-time $49 verification fee — this keeps the directory honest and filters out restaurants that don't actually qualify.

OpenTable vs Resy — which is better?

OpenTable wins on sheer volume — 60,000+ restaurants worldwide. Resy wins for trendy, chef-driven spots that OpenTable doesn't carry. If you're booking a standard nice dinner, OpenTable. If you're chasing the hot new place, Resy.
