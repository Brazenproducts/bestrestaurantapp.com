2
Yelp
Crowdsourced reviews and local discovery
★★★★☆ 4.3/5

Yelp remains a solid choice for discovering restaurants based on real user reviews. Its massive database and review ecosystem make it invaluable for finding hidden gems. However, ad-heavy listings and occasional review manipulation concerns keep it from the top spot.

📝 User Reviews 📸 Photo Gallery 🔍 Filters & Search 📍 Nearby Spots
3
OpenTable
The reservation standard
★★★★☆ 4.2/5

OpenTable excels at what it does best: restaurant reservations. With a vast network of partnered restaurants and a reliable booking system, it's the go-to for planning ahead. The points/rewards system is a nice bonus, but it's strictly a reservation tool—no delivery, no price transparency features.

📅 Easy Reservations 🎁 Rewards Points 📊 Availability 💬 Diner Reviews
4
DoorDash
Largest U.S. delivery network
★★★★☆ 4.0/5

DoorDash leads the delivery space with the widest restaurant selection and fastest delivery times. DashPass offers decent savings for frequent users. The downsides: inflated menu prices, service fees, and the ever-present pressure to tip your driver generously on top of everything else.

🚗 Fast Delivery 🏪 Wide Selection 💳 DashPass Savings 📦 Grocery Too
5
Uber Eats
Integrated with your Uber account
★★★☆☆ 3.8/5

Uber Eats benefits from Uber's logistics infrastructure and its built-in user base. The Uber One membership bundles ride and food savings. However, fees can stack up quickly, and the tip-prompting experience has become increasingly aggressive. Restaurant coverage varies significantly by city.

🔗 Uber Integration 🌍 Global Reach 📱 Uber One Bundle ⏱ Real-Time Tracking
6
GrubHub
A pioneer showing its age
★★★☆☆ 3.5/5

GrubHub was once the delivery king but has fallen behind competitors. Restaurant selection has narrowed, the app experience feels dated, and fee transparency remains poor. The Amazon Prime partnership adds some value, but it's not enough to overcome a generally declining user experience.
